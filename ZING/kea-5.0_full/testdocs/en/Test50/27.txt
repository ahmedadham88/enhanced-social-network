Many of these enclosures were accomplished by acts of Parliament in the 16th and 17th centuries. Some of the practices of enclosures were denounced by the Church, and legislation was drawn up against it; but the developments in agricultural mechanization from the 16th to 18th centuries required large, enclosed fields to be successfully workable to provide more food for all.
