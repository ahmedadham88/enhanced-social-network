People who take up fitness later in life are more likely to "age successfully" and stay healthy compared with those who remain couch potatoes, a large British study finds.
Researchers tracked the health of 3,454 people in England with an average age of 64 living in the communityThey defined healthy aging as being free of major chronic ailments such as heart disease or stroke, diabetes, emphysema, or Alzheimer's disease as well as having good mental health, preserving cognitive abilities, and being able to maintain social connections or activities over the eight years of followup.
